Threat number two: Tool Misuse and Exploitation.

In the 2025 LLM Top Ten this sat under Sensitive Information Disclosure, where the model accidentally leaked P I I, credentials, or proprietary training data in its text output.

Agents change the shape of the problem. They are given real tools on purpose — A P Is, file systems, mail, databases — and they abuse those tools in ways the developer never modelled. The call succeeds, the logs look clean, and the agent has just emailed your customer list to an attacker.

My top mitigation is Azure A P I Management, configured as a GenAI gateway. Every tool call is validated, rate-limited, and policy-checked, and it gives you a single point of observability.

Pair that with Microsoft Purview data loss prevention, so even a legitimate tool call cannot exfiltrate sensitive content.
